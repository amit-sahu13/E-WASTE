ABOUT E-WASTE

Electronic waste, or e-waste, refers to discarded electronic devices and electrical equipment. This category includes everything from old smartphones and laptops to refrigerators and televisions. E-waste poses a significant environmental and health hazard when not managed responsibly.

GROWING E-WASTE PROBLEM

With the rapid advancement of technology, the lifespan of electronic devices has shortened considerably. As a result, the amount of e-waste generated worldwide has skyrocketed. Unfortunately, much of this waste ends up in landfills or is improperly disposed of, leading to pollution, resource depletion, and health risks.

EFFECTS ON ENVIRONMENT

ENVIRONMENTAL POLLUTION

a) Toxic Chemicals: Many electronic devices contain hazardous materials such as lead, mercury, cadmium, and brominated flame retardants. When e-waste is not disposed of properly, these chemicals can leach into the soil and water, leading to soil contamination and water pollution.

b) Air Pollution: Burning or incinerating e-waste releases harmful toxins and pollutants into the air, contributing to air pollution and posing a risk to respiratory health.

ENVIRONMENTAL POLLUTION

a) Soil Contamination: Improper disposal of e-waste in landfills can contaminate the soil with hazardous substances. This contamination can affect the growth of plants and can potentially enter the food chain, posing health risks to humans and wildlife.

b) Water Pollution: When e-waste is dumped in landfills or disposed of inappropriately, rainwater can wash toxins from the waste into rivers, lakes, and groundwater, leading to water pollution. This can harm aquatic life and make water sources unsafe for consumptiont here.

EFFECTS ON HUMAN HEALTH

Electronic waste contains toxic components that are dangerous to human health, such as mercury, lead, cadmium, polybrominated flame retardants, barium and lithium. The negative health effects of these toxins on humans include brain, heart, liver, kidney and skeletal system damage. It can also considerably affect the nervous and reproductive systems of the human body, leading to disease and birth defects. Improper disposal of e-waste is unbelievably dangerous to the global environment, which is why it is so important to spread awareness on this growing problem and the threatening aftermath.

STATISTICAL - ANALYSIS

  • China, USA , and India produce the most e-waste.
  • Only 17.4% of e-waste is known to be collected and properly recycled
  • There is over 347 Mt of unrecycled e-waste on earth in 2023
  • The e-waste recycling market was valued at $49,880 million in 2020.
